## First Steps (Turns 1-100)

### Initial State
When you start your kingdom, you have:
- **Population:** 50,000
- **Gold:** 10,000
- **Food:** 5,000
- **Mana:** 5,000
- **Land:** 4,225
- **Buildings:** 10 farms, 1 barracks, 1 school, 1 armory, 1 market, 100 housing
- **Troops:** 50 rangers, 100 researchers, 100 engineers
- **Turns Stored:** 400

### What Happens Every Turn
Each turn automatically:
1. **Resources are generated** - Gold from economy, food from farms, mana from research
2. **Population grows** - If food and housing are sufficient
3. **Morale updates** - Based on food, happiness, prosperity
4. **Troops consume resources** - Each unit requires gold upkeep
5. **Buildings progress** - Construction and research advance
6. **Markets fluctuate** - Prices change for trading

### Turn 1-10: Observe the Baseline
**Goal:** Understand your economy without making changes

- Take 10 turns and observe what happens
- Watch your gold increase ~25,000 per turn
- Notice population growth of ~185-200 per turn
- Food surplus is building up
- Happiness should be 50-70 range

**Key Metric:** Gold per turn is your primary resource generator. At the start, expect +20k to +25k per turn.

### Turn 10-100: First Economic Decisions

**Your First Priority: Food Security**

The game begins with only 5,000 food stored. Let's verify we can survive comfortably:
- At 50,000 population, food consumption is ~100-200 per turn
- With food building surplus (1,000+/turn from farms), you're safe for now
- Build 2-3 more farms to establish a safety margin

**Housing Consideration:**

You have 100 housing slots. Your population (50,000) is already filling housing capacity. Before the population grows past capacity:
- Each housing unit = 500 population capacity
- Build 5-10 more housing (for 2,500-5,000 more population)
- Don't over-build housing early - it's expensive in resources

**Troops to Maintain:**

You start with 150 military units (50 rangers + 100 support):
- Upkeep is ~150 gold/turn for all troops
- This is negligible (0.2-0.3% of income)
- Keep them for now - they'll be useful

**What NOT to do:**

- Don't build expensive buildings yet (Vaults, Mage Towers, Libraries)
- Don't train expensive troops (Mages, Clerics, Thieves)
- Don't start research yet
- This is exploration phase

### End of Turn 100 Checkpoint

**Expected State:**
- Gold: 1,000,000+
- Population: 53,000-55,000
- Food: 8,000-10,000
- Housing: 12-15 buildings
- Farms: 12-15 buildings

## Building Your Economy (Turns 100-500)

### Turn 100-200: Establish Resource Infrastructure

You're now wealthy. It's time to invest in your economy.

**Markets are Your Friends**

Markets generate prosperity (happiness) and enable trading. Build your first market if you haven't already.
- Markets also unlock trading routes later
- Every market increases prosperity by +2% per market

**Resource Production Buildings**

You need multiple resource types for advanced buildings:
- **Vaults** (store excess gold safely)
- **Granaries** (store food, prevents spoilage)
- **Smithies** (create tools for faster building)

Build 1-2 of each. Don't overdo it yet.

### Turn 200-300: Basic Research

Now that you're generating stable income, start researching. This unlocks:
- Better troop types
- Advanced buildings
- Spell systems
- Better resource generation

**Which Techs First?**

- **Weapons Research** - Improves ranger damage
- **Armor Research** - Improves all troop defenses
- **Military Tactics** - Unlocks better unit types
- **Construction** - Faster building times

Don't research spell techs yet - you need Mage Towers first.

### Turn 300-500: Scale Up

You now have a solid economic base. It's time to grow:
- Build more markets (target 3-5 total)
- Expand housing (target 20-30 buildings)
- Build schools for research speed bonus
- Train more researchers (they provide XP without combat)

**Happiness & Morale Management**

At this point, you've noticed:
- **Morale** (kingdom-wide, 0-120) affects troop morale
- **Happiness** (population mood, 0-100) affects growth

How to increase both:
- Prosperity (markets, trade): +4/turn
- Food surplus: +6/turn  
- Safety (low attacks): +20/turn
- Recovery: +0.2/turn
- Race bonuses: varies

Target: Keep morale 60+ and happiness 70+

### Turn 500 Checkpoint

**Expected State:**
- Gold: 2,000,000+
- Population: 60,000-65,000
- Housing: 25-30 buildings
- Markets: 3-5 buildings
- Farms: 15-20 buildings
- Granaries: 2-3 buildings
- Schools: 2-3 buildings
- Troops: 50-200 researchers and engineers
- Research: 3-4 techs completed

## Creating Your Army (Turns 500-1500)

### Turn 500-800: Unlock Military Infrastructure

You can't fight with just rangers. Time to build military capacity:

**Buildings to Add:**
- **Barracks** (train fighters, rangers, thieves, ninjas)
- **Armories** (train weapon-focused troops)
- **Training Grounds** (speed up training)
- **Guard Towers** (defensive structures, increase morale)

Build 2-5 barracks and 2-3 armories.

**Unit Economics**

Different troops cost different amounts:
- **Fighters:** 150 gold each (expensive, strong)
- **Rangers:** 50 gold each (cheaper, medium strength)
- **Clerics:** 200 gold each (heal, expensive)
- **Mages:** 300+ gold each (require spell research)
- **Thieves:** 250 gold each (covert ops)

**Early Strategy:** Train 200-500 fighters. This is your offensive/defensive backbone.

### Turn 800-1200: Build Your Army

Now that you have barracks, start serious military development:

**Training Allocation**

The game uses "Allocation" - you set a number of units to train per turn:
- Set fighters allocation to 10-20/turn
- Continue researchers (they're cheap)
- Experiment with clerics (10-20/turn for healing backup)

At 10 fighters/turn = 300 fighters per month = 3,600 per year

**Resource Support for Troops**

Troops need resources:
- Weapons stockpile (scales with fighters)
- Armor stockpile (scales with clerics & armored units)
- Meal cost (150 gold/turn baseline)

Monitor your resource builds to support your growing army.

### Turn 1200-1500: Military Optimization

By now you have 500-1000 fighters. Start thinking about balance:

**Mixed Unit Composition**

- 60% Fighters (your core)
- 20% Clerics (healing, morale)
- 10% Rangers (ranged damage)
- 10% Other (specialists)

**Population vs Military**

Remember: Large armies consume resources. Don't train more than you can support:
- 1000 fighters = ~150+ gold/turn upkeep
- Make sure your gold income exceeds this comfortably

At turn 1500, you should have:
- Gold income: 200k+/turn
- Army: 500-1500 units total
- Treasury: 5,000,000+ gold

## Knowledge is Power (Turns 1500-2500)

### Turn 1500-1800: Unlock Spell Systems

You now have resources to invest in magical research:

**Prerequisites:**
- Mage Towers (expensive, ~50,000 gold + resources each)
- Spell Research techs
- Mages trained (expensive at 300+ gold each)

**Which School First?**

- **Offensive Magic** - Attack/damage spells
- **Defensive Magic** - Protection/shielding
- **Support Magic** - Healing, buffs

Offensive magic is most immediately useful.

**Spell Casting:**

Once you have mages and spells researched, you can cast spells:
- Costs Mana (~50-500 per spell)
- Some spells damage enemies directly
- Some buff your kingdom
- Some weaken enemy defenses

### Turn 1800-2200: Advanced Building & Research

Your economy is strong. Time to unlock advanced features:

**Libraries** - Unlock exotic research paths
**Shrines** - Divine powers, morale bonuses, special units
**Smithies** - Create tools for faster building
**Mausoleums** - Necromancy and undead units (advanced)

**Advanced Research Paths:**

- Spell research (offensive, defensive, support, divine)
- Exotic resources (coal, steel, rare materials)
- Fortifications (walls, towers, advanced defense)
- Trade routes & economics

### Turn 2200-2500: Specialization Decision

By now, you have to choose your kingdom's focus:

**Pick ONE or TWO:**
1. **Military Domination** - Max out fighters, focus on combat
2. **Economic Powerhouse** - Max markets, trade routes, prosperity
3. **Magical Kingdom** - Max mages, spell research, towers
4. **Fortress Kingdom** - Max defensive structures, low population
5. **Balanced Kingdom** - Medium of everything

There's no "wrong" choice - playstyle depends on preference.

## Common Issues & Solutions

### "I'm Running Out of Gold"
- Your expenses exceed income
- Reduce troop upkeep (disband units)
- Increase gold generation (build markets)
- Cut resource production if you have surplus

### "My Population Stopped Growing"
- You're at housing capacity
- Build more housing (100 pop per housing building)
- Ensure food surplus is positive
- Morale must be 50+ for growth

### "I'm Losing Too Much Food"
- You have more population than farms support
- Build more granaries (prevent spoilage)
- Build more farms (increase production)
- Reduce population if needed (cull troops)

### "My Troops Keep Getting Hurt"
- You're being attacked or taking friendly damage
- Build guard towers & fortifications
- Keep morale high (troops fight better)
- Move troops out of danger areas if possible

### "Techs Take Forever to Research"
- You need more researchers
- Train 200-500 researchers
- Build more schools (research speed bonus)
- Focus research on fewer techs simultaneously

## Game Mechanics Reference

### Turn Cycle
Each turn:
1. Resource generation
2. Troop upkeep/consumption
3. Population growth
4. Building/research progression
5. Event processing
6. Score update

### Resource Types

**Primary:**
- **Gold** - Main currency, everything costs gold
- **Population** - Your workforce, generates resources
- **Food** - Sustains population, generated by farms
- **Mana** - Magic resource, regenerates 3/turn

**Secondary:**
- **Research Resources** - Economy, Weapons, Armor, Military, etc.
- **Tools** - Hammers, Scaffolding, Blueprints (for faster building)
- **Stockpiles** - Weapons, Armor (military supplies)

### Building Types

**Economy:**
- Farm, Granary, Vault, Market, Bank, Tavern

**Military:**
- Barracks, Armory, Training Grounds, Guard Tower, Castle, Wall

**Research:**
- School, Library, Mage Tower, Shrine

**Special:**
- Housing, Gate, Outpost, Smithy, Mausoleum

### Troop Types

| Unit | Cost | Upkeep | Strength | Notes |
|------|------|--------|----------|-------|
| Fighter | 150 | 1 | High | Core military unit |
| Ranger | 50 | 1 | Medium | Cheap, ranged |
| Cleric | 200 | 1 | Low | Healing support |
| Mage | 300+ | 1 | Vary | Spell caster |
| Thief | 250 | 1 | Medium | Covert ops |
| Ninja | Vary | 1 | High | Advanced fighter |

### Happiness & Morale

**Happiness** affects:
- Population growth rate
- Troop effectiveness
- Research speed

**Morale** affects:
- Army morale in combat
- Unit training speed
- Overall stability

**How to Increase:**
- Prosperity (markets)
- Food surplus
- Low casualty rates
- Recovery (natural, +0.2/turn)
- Racial bonuses

## Tips & Tricks

1. **Early Game:** Don't waste gold on expensive buildings. Focus on basics.
2. **Mid Game:** Establish specialization early. It defines your late-game.
3. **Late Game:** Optimize for your chosen path. Small gains add up.
4. **Always:** Keep food surplus positive. Starvation is death.
5. **Always:** Monitor gold income. It's your lifeline.
6. **Research:** Don't ignore it. It unlocks everything.
7. **Population:** More isn't always better. Balance with resources.
8. **Defense:** Build early. Attacks hurt.
